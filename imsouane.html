Surfing Imsouane

Where time slows down and the waves roll forever

Why Imsouane is a Surfer’s Paradise

Imsouane is not just a surf spot — it’s a state of mind. This small Moroccan fishing village has become world-renowned for having one of the longest right-hand point breaks on Earth. It’s the kind of place where you come for a weekend and stay for a month.

The Bay vs Cathedral

The Bay is gentle, glassy, and endless — perfect for longboarders and beginners. Cathedral offers a more exposed, punchy wave for intermediate surfers seeking some challenge.

When to Go

Peak season runs from September to March, when Atlantic swells light up the coastline. But even in summer, the waves are consistent, clean, and welcoming.

Surf Lessons & Rentals

Looking to learn or improve? Olas Surf Morocco offers surf coaching, rentals, and guided sessions — run by locals who know the tides and the timing.

Imsouane vs Taghazout

Taghazout is lively, crowded, and full of surf camps. Imsouane is slower, calmer, and more authentic. It’s the perfect place to reconnect with the ocean and yourself.

Where to Stay

Olas Surf House, cliffside apartments, rooftop hostels — Imsouane is packed with soulful stays, great views, and strong WiFi for nomads who ride waves and work.

Final Stoke

Imsouane is more than a destination — it’s a vibe. Long waves. Golden sunsets. Fishermen on the shore. It’s the kind of magic that stays with you long after you leave.
